Bridge

A bridge is used when 1-4 teeth are missing but there are at least 2 teeth to act as support for the artificial tooth, or teeth, on the bridge. For example, if you have 3 teeth (A-B-C) and tooth B is missing, an artificial tooth will be made with crowns on either side of it. One crown will be cemented to tooth A and another to tooth C with the artificial one taking the place of missing tooth B. It is important to note that a bridge is different from a flipper or a partial because it cannot be removed. A bridge is priced using our crown price. So if you wanted a 3-unit bridge like in our A-B-C example the cost would be priced at $985.00 per tooth in the unit which results in a price of $2,955.00.

Occlusal guard

An occlusal guard is often referred to as a night guard and can come in hard, soft, or a combination of hard and soft. An occlusal guard is worn at night to help prevent wear on the surface of the teeth caused by clenching or grinding. In some cases it can help to alleviate jaw pain associated with clenching your teeth during sleep, as well. Choosing the right occlusal guard for you will be best determined by a short evaluation with the dentist.
